How Harriet Tubman Is Saving Black Women's Lives

The founders of GirlTrek are channeling African-American pride to tackle the health crisis among black women, one march at a time.

Catherine Cheney

NationSwell editor and Multimedia journalist http:www.catcheney.com

The trouble started in grade school. Mom had changed shifts at the local hosiery factory. Dad was working long hours as a mechanic. Big brother had left town after enlisting in the army. So Vonda Vass Summers would come home each day to an empty house in Henderson, North Carolina. Alone and bored, Vonda found solace in bologna sandwiches and boxes of mac n' cheese. She binged, then hid the evidence, hoping no one would see what she'd done.
